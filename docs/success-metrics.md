# Success Metrics and Decision Rules

## 1. Success Metrics

| Metric ID | Metric | Target | How to Measure | Related Requirement |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| M-01 | Homepage understanding rate | 80% or higher | Users who correctly explain the purpose of RSU Nexus after viewing the homepage / total users | FR-01 |
| M-02 | Task completion rate | 80% or higher | Users who successfully complete the main workflow (search → view listing → submit request) / total users | FR-02 |
| M-03 | Listing submission accuracy | 80% or higher | Valid listing submissions / total listing submissions | FR-03, FR-10 |
| M-04 | Search and filter success rate | 80% or higher | Users who successfully find a required resource or student service using search and filters / total users | FR-05, FR-06 |
| M-05 | Status understanding rate | 80% or higher | Users who correctly understand listing availability and request-status labels / total users | FR-07, FR-08 |
| M-06 | Usability rating | Average 4 out of 5 | Tester ratings collected through the feedback form | FR-13 |
| M-07 | Overall usefulness rating | Average 4 out of 5 | Tester ratings collected through the feedback form | MVP Features |
| M-08 | User adoption intention | 80% or higher | Users who answer "Yes" when asked if they would use RSU Nexus if it were available | Value Proposition |

## 2. Decision Rules

| Result | Decision |
|---|---|
| All or most success metrics meet the target | Continue with full implementation and finalize the prototype using the validated design and workflow. |
| Some metrics do not meet the target | Revise the wireframes, navigation, labels, search/filter functions, forms, or feature priorities before continuing implementation. |
| Most metrics do not meet the target | Re-evaluate the problem statement, user stories, MVP scope, and critical assumptions, then redesign and repeat usability testing before further development. |
